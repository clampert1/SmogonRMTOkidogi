TEAM BUILDING PROCESS
Okidogi (Asgore)
I chose to create a team around Okidogi because you hardly ever see it and it has so much potential, especially as an anti-lead. It is the ultimate Landorus-Therian killer. Okidogi has great stats, an amazing moveset, even better typing, and a killer ability; Guard Dog.
+ Corviknight (Mettaton)
Corviknight goes hand in hand with Okidogi, resisting everything that is super-effective towards Okidogi and not to mention ground immunity. Corviknight is a natural physical bulky Pokemon, allowing you to hard-switch into it with little punishment.
+ Ting-Lu (Annoying Dog)
Ting-Lu is the other end of the spectrum with astronomically great special defensive capabilities. Ting-Lu provides psychic immunity, something Okidogi is 4x weak to, threatens stealth rocks and ruination, and provides the only ground type move in this entire team; Earthquake.
+ Volcarona (Reaper Bird)
Volcarona sits here as a mid-to-late game setup sweeper. I used a meta moveset with a higher priority towards special attack rather than speed.
+ Kyurem (Temmie)
Here, Kyurem plays to gain immediate speed-control and recover pressure. Kyurem holds a choice scarf and max special attack, the goal being to regain control and threaten hyper-offensive teams.
+ Lopunny-Mega (Mad Mew Mew)
Lopunny-Mega is the glue that holds this team together that functions very well with Corviknight, alongside the other members of the team. Lopunny-Mega provides that extra manpower needed to bust down teams, alongside a strong and reliable priority move.

Okidogi plays as an anti-lead, mid-game punisher, and a sack options in most late-games. Okidogi's ability Guard Dog raises the user's Attack stat +1 for each intimidation, instead of lowering the user's Attack stat -1. This immediately changes up a lot of encounters, especially against Landorus-Therian, a common lead Pokemon. Okidogi sports Ice Punch against meta Pokemon such as Landorus-Therian, Garchomp and Gliscor. Being able to kill offensive Landorus-Therian and Garchomp. Gunk shot is Okidogi's best stab option, being able to roughly 3 hit KO a fully defensive Alomomola with the added chance of Poisoning. Close Combat is Okidogi's second stab option, allowing great coverage. Rock Tomb is added as coverage, especially against Charizard-Mega Y, Volcarona and Moltres. Next, Okidogi's EVs is calculated perfectly to be able to OHKO offensive Landorus-Therian, Garchomp, Charizard-Mega Y and Volcarona. Okidogi can also survive most meta movesets from Landorus-Therian (Except Flyinium Z), Garchomp, Charizard-Mega Y, Great Tusk, Kingambit, Iron Valiant (phys/special), Ogerpon-Wellspring and most other popular OU mons. Lastly, Shuca Berry allows Okidogi to survive ground-type moves, the most common threat you will encounter. I used Shuca berry over Air Balloon for longevity and to bait the opponent into clicking a ground-type move, thinking they will OHKO Okidogi.

Volcarona is the only set-up sweeper on this team. I did not have a specific reason why I chose Volcarona other than it being a great nuisance to most teams once you reach mid-to-late game. This Volcarona focuses on max Special Attack over Speed to maximize it's damage. It is important to note I had zero encounters where I was punished for not maximizing speed, but being in a very competitive speed stat of 100, there is an argument that maximizing Speed is more important. Additionally I mentioned that Ting-Lu has the only ground-type move in this team, but that is a lie. The theory behind Hidden Power Ground is to surprise counter Heatran, who would Wall Volcarona, but in all of my games I had not used Hidden Power Ground once. In fact, if swapped out for Giga Drain, Volcarona would have provided more useful than it is now. It really is a personal preference to choose Giga Drain or Hidden Power Ground, but I chose to stuck with Hidden Power Ground despite not using it.

THREAT LIST
CORVIKNIGHT
This team doesn't have a clear answer against defensive Corviknight. Brave Bird is able to accumulate chip damage on Pokemon that might not want to be chipped.
ARAQUANID
This team can't necessarily OHKO Araquanid, and will often take massive damage, or require a Pokemon to be sacked just to chip it in OHKO range.
ALOMOMOLA
Assault Vest Alomomola + Mirror Coat can usually OHKO would-be counters on this team like Kyurem, making it annoying to KO with Lopunny-Mega and Okidogi, leaving them vulnerable to being chipped and burned with scald.
